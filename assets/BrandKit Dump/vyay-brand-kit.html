Group expenses, settled. 01

Logo system

One identity, two registers. The hero mark — the textured, embossed coin — carries warmth and craft at large sizes. The flat system does the daily work: it survives 16 px, ships as vectors, and recolors with CSS. Neither replaces the other; the mistake would be asking one render to do both jobs.

HERO · ≥128 px only

Hero mark

Splash screen, store listing, launch marketing, social hero images. Never in app chrome, navigation, or below 128 px — the texture and emboss collapse into noise.

PRIMARY · everywhere else

Primary badge

The same composition, flattened: Indigo 600 coin, the stitch ring, the wordmark. This is what the product, favicon pipeline, and partners use.

Variants

Construction rules

Clear spaceKeep 25% of the mark’s height clear on all sides. Nothing enters this zone — not taglines, not partner logos.
Minimum sizesBadge 24 px · wordmarks 16 px tall · hero mark 128 px. Below 24 px, switch to the compact व्य glyph.
ColorBadge sits on white, Neutral 1000, or photography behind a scrim. Wordmarks are currentColor: ink on light, white on dark, never mid-grays.
App iconFull-bleed tile, no ring. Android adaptive icons crop to a 66/108 safe zone — the ring would clip; the naked wordmark doesn’t.

Color

The hero render leaned blue; the app leaned violet. The system settles on the hue between them — indigo — and commits. Indigo 600 is the brand on light surfaces; Indigo 400 carries it on dark. Marigold is the single warm accent, reserved for celebration: rewards, streaks, milestones. Everything else is neutral so the money colors can speak.

Fintech semantic system

Fifteen meanings, eight hue families — the reuse is deliberate. A money app that gives every state its own hue becomes a fruit salad; states that share meaning share a family and differ by component. The rules that keep them apart: Expense rose is not Error red — spending is normal, failure is not. Pending vs Warning: same amber, but Pending is a soft chip with a clock; Warning is a filled alert with a triangle. Processing vs Info: same sky, Processing moves (spinner), Info doesn’t. Budget is a traffic light: emerald under 80%, amber to 100%, red beyond. And color never works alone — every credit carries +/↗, every debit −/↘, so the system survives red-green color blindness. Each card below shows live light and dark tokens; the AA chips are computed contrast ratios, not aspirations.

Typography

Two jobs, two faces — and both scripts treated as equals. Anek (Ek Type) is the display voice: a Latin + Devanagari superfamily drawn together, so “Vyay” and “व्यय” share bones instead of being strangers. Inter is the working text face: unmatched screen hinting and true tabular figures — the feature that keeps columns of rupees aligned. Devanagari body text falls back to Noto Sans Devanagari, Inter’s metric neighbor.

DISPLAY · Anek 600
Vyay — settle up, stay friends.
हिसाब बराबर।

Headlines, amounts on marketing surfaces, section headers. The Devanagari specimen doubles as a strapline candidate: hisaab barabar — “the account is even.”

TEXT · Inter
You spent ₹8,450 across 12 transactions, about 15% more than October. Food was your largest category.

All UI and body copy, 400–650 weight. Set font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums lining-nums on anything that carries an amount.

Usage

Do

  • Use the hero mark large and rarely — splash, store, launch posts.
  • Recolor wordmarks via currentColor; they are built for it.
  • Pair every colored amount with a sign or arrow glyph.
  • Use Marigold only when something good happened.
  • Use the English badge where Devanagari has no audience context.

Don’t

  • Put the textured render in navigation, chips, or below 128 px.
  • Use Error red for ordinary debits — that’s Expense rose’s job.
  • Add shadows, glows, or gradients to the flat marks.
  • Stretch, outline, or re-space the Devanagari letterforms.
  • Introduce new hues; extend by reusing families.
